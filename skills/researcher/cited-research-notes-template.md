# Cited Research Notes Template
> The shape of a distilled, sourced research note.

Fill every slot; keep the whole note under one screen.

- **Question** — the exact question this note answers, one line.
- **Answer (TL;DR)** — 1–3 sentences, decision-ready, stated up front.
- **Key findings** — 3–6 bullets. Each bullet: one claim, then its source in brackets [Source name, date]. One claim per bullet; no source, no bullet.
- **Comparison / numbers** — optional small table or bullets when weighing options; cite each row.
- **Confidence** — High / Medium / Low, plus the single biggest reason (e.g., "single vendor source", "corroborated by 3 independents").
- **Open questions** — what remains unverified or needs a follow-up search.
- **Sources** — numbered list: title, publisher, date, and why it was trusted (one phrase each).

## Rules
- Distill, never paste raw pages; quote at most one short line per source.
- Date the note itself; mark anything time-sensitive.
- If sources conflict, show both sides and say which you weight and why.
